Introduction to Kinematics

Kinematics is the branch of mechanics that describes the motion of objects without considering the causes of motion. It involves concepts such as displacement, velocity, and acceleration.

Key Terms:

  • Displacement: The change in position of an object.
  • Velocity: The rate of change of displacement.
  • Acceleration: The rate of change of velocity.

Understanding these concepts is essential for analyzing how objects move in various situations.

One-Dimensional Motion

One-dimensional motion refers to movement along a straight line, such as a car moving along a straight road.

Equations of Motion:

  • Displacement: Δx = xf - xi
  • Velocity: v = Δx / Δt
  • Acceleration: a = Δv / Δt
  • Final Velocity: vf = vi + aΔt
  • Displacement with Constant Acceleration: Δx = viΔt + ½a(Δt)2

Real-World Example:

Imagine a car accelerating from rest. We can use the equations above to determine its velocity and position at any given time.

Interactive Exercises

Apply what you've learned by solving these interactive problems.

Exercise 1: Calculating Final Velocity

A car starts from rest and accelerates at 5 m/s² for 8 seconds. What is its final velocity?

Hint: Use the equation v = v₀ + at, where v₀ is the initial velocity (0 in this case).

Exercise 2: Calculating Maximum Height

A ball is thrown vertically upward with an initial velocity of 20 m/s. Calculate its maximum height. (Assume acceleration due to gravity is -10 m/s²)

Hint: Use the equation v² = v₀² + 2a(Δx). At the highest point, v = 0.
